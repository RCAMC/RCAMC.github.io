A Comfort Shared-Control Framework for Legged Robots

Zhikai Wang1, Junyi Shi2, Liang Ding1, XX XX1,
1State Key Lab of Robotics & System, Harbin Institute of Technology
2Aalto University
Paper Long Video

Abstract

In addition to the advantages of flexibility and strong passing capability, legged robots with high degrees of freedom also bring challenges to control. While fully autonomous control is the goal that researchers are striving to achieve, shared control that conforms to human cognition is a more reliable solution that better combines human high-dimensional decision-making capabilities and robotic algorithmic intelligence when perception and autonomous learning capabilities are not yet mature. However, the current shared control for legged robots is only limited to simple direct control of forward direction, posture, and single-leg motion, which is very tedious and requires experienced operators. In this paper, we propose a comfortable shared control framework for legged robots to address this problem. The framework requires only a rough navigation direction or path sketch provided by a human, and the algorithm can smooth and optimize the navigation path according to the human's intention, making the path have a smaller traversal cost. Furthermore, The proposed fastMCTS is used as an evaluation algorithm to assess the traversability of the generated paths. The results are fed through an intuitive image interface and force perception, and the operator makes decisions based on the results avoiding complex contact state planning. Finally, we validate the evaluation algorithm, the planning results and the comfort of the framework on simulation and real-world experiments.
